Stephen the Great

Prince of Moldavia

Stephen the Great, also known as Stephen III of Moldavia, was a famous 15th century ruler of the Principality of Moldavia, a region located in present-day Romania, Moldova, and Ukraine. He is widely considered one of the greatest leaders in Romanian history and is remembered for his military victories, religious devotion, and cultural achievements.

At the age of 18, he succeeded his uncle as Prince of Moldavia and quickly established himself as a powerful and ambitious ruler.

One of Stephen's main priorities was to defend Moldavia from the constant threat of invasion from the Ottoman Empire. He led several successful military campaigns against the Ottomans, earning him the nickname "Stephen the Great." He also formed alliances with other European powers, such as Poland and Hungary, to further strengthen Moldavia's defense.
